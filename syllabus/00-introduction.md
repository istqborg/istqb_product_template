# Introduction

## Purpose of this Syllabus

This syllabus forms the basis for the International Software Testing Qualification for Digital Accessibility Testing at Specialist level. The ISTQB® provides this syllabus as follows:

1. To member boards, to translate into their local language and to accredit training providers. Member boards may adapt the syllabus to their particular language needs and modify the references to adapt to their local publications.
2. To certification bodies, to derive examination questions in their local language adapted to the learning objectives for this syllabus.
3. To training providers, to produce training materials and determine appropriate teaching methods.
4. To certification candidates, to prepare for the certification exam (either as part of a training course or independently).
5. To the international software and systems engineering community, to advance software and systems testing profession, and as a basis for books and articles.

## The Digital Accessibility Testing Qualification

The Digital Accessibility Testing qualification is aimed at anyone involved in software testing who wishes to broaden their knowledge of digital accessibility testing or anyone who wishes to start a specialist career in digital accessibility testing. The qualification is also aimed at anyone involved in software development who wishes to understand digital accessibility testing.

## Career Path for Testers

The ISTQB® scheme supports testing professionals at all stages of their careers offering both breadth and depth of knowledge. Individuals who achieve the ISTQB® Digital Accessibility Testing certification may also be interested in the Usability Testing certification. \[ISTQB-CT-US\]

## Business Outcomes {.business-outcomes}

This section lists the Business Outcomes expected of a candidate who has achieved the Accessibility Testing certification.

An Accessibility Testing Certified Tester can...

1. DAT-BO1 Explain the value of digital accessibility principles and their impact on users.
1. DAT-BO2 Interpret global guidelines to apply accessibility standards.
1. DAT-BO3 Define accessibility testing activities
1. DAT-BO4 Perform, analyze, and report accessibility defects (or anomalies)
1. DAT-BO5 Use assistive technology for accessibility testing
1. DAT-BO6 Select and use tools for accessibility testing

## Learning Objectives and Cognitive Level of Knowledge

Learning Objectives support the business outcomes and are used to create the Accessibility Testing exams.

In general, all contents of this syllabus are examinable, except for the Introduction and Appendices. The exam questions will confirm knowledge of keywords at K1 level (see below) or learning objectives at the respective level of knowledge.

The specific learning objectives and their levels of knowledge are shown at the beginning of each chapter, and classified as follows:

* K1: Remember
* K2: Understand
* K3: Apply
* K4: Analyze

Further details and examples of learning objectives are given in Appendix A.

For all terms listed as keywords just below chapter headings, the correct name and definition from the ISTQB® glossary shall be remembered (K1), even if not explicitly mentioned in the learning objective.

## The Digital Accessibility Testing Certificate Exam

The Digital Accessibility Testing Certificate exam will be based on this syllabus. Answers to exam questions may require the use of material based on more than one section of this syllabus. All sections of the syllabus are examinable, except for the Introduction and Appendices. Standards and books are included as references, but their content is not examinable, beyond what is summarized in the syllabus itself from such standards and books.

Refer to Exam Structures and Rules document for the Accessibility Testing module for further details.

The entry criterion for taking the Accessibility Testing Specialist Level exam is that candidates are interested in software usability testing. However, it is strongly recommended that candidates also:

> • Have at least a minimal background in either software development or software testing, such as six months experience as a system or user acceptance tester or as a software developer
>
> • Take a course that has been accredited to ISTQB® standards (by one of the ISTQB-recognized member boards).

Entry Requirement Note: The ISTQB® Foundation Level certificate shall be obtained before taking the Digital Accessibility Testing certification exam.

## Accreditation

An ISTQB® Member Board may accredit training providers whose course material follows this syllabus. Training providers should obtain accreditation guidelines from the Member Board or body that performs the accreditation. An accredited course is recognized as conforming to this syllabus and is allowed to have an ISTQB® exam as part of the course.

The accreditation guidelines for this syllabus follow the general Accreditation Guidelines published by the Processes Management and Compliance Working Group.

## Handling of Standards

International standardization organizations like IEEE and ISO have issued standards associated with quality characteristics and software testing. Such standards are referenced in this syllabus. The purpose of these references is to provide a framework (as in the references to ISO 25010 regarding quality characteristics) or to provide a source of additional information if desired by the reader. Please note that ISTQB® syllabi are using the standard documents as reference. Standards documents are not intended for examination. Refer to section 7 - References for more information on Standards.

## Level of Detail

The level of detail in this syllabus allows internationally consistent courses and exams. To achieve this goal, the syllabus consists of:

* General instructional objectives describing the intention of the Digital Accessibility Testing, Specialist Level
* A list of terms that students must be able to recall
* Learning objectives for each knowledge area, describing the cognitive learning outcome to be achieved
* A description of the key concepts, including references to sources such as accepted literature or standards

The syllabus content does not describe the entire knowledge area of software accessibility testing; it reflects the level of detail to be covered in Digital Accessibility Testing, Specialist Level training courses. It focuses on test approaches and test techniques that can apply to all software projects, including those following Agile software development.

The syllabus uses the terminology (i.e. the name and meaning) of the terms used in software testing and quality assurance according to the ISTQB® Glossary (ISTQB® Glossary, Chapter 7.3)

For the terminology in related disciplines please refer to the respective glossaries: IREB-CPRE for requirements engineering (IREB-CPRE, Chapter 7.3), and Agile Alliance for agile terminology (Agile Alliance, Chapter 7.3)

## How this Syllabus is Organized

There are six chapters with examinable content. The top-level heading for each chapter specifies the time for the chapter; timing is not provided below the chapter level. *For accredited training courses, the syllabus requires a minimum of* \<number\> hours of instruction, distributed across the seven chapters as follows:

* Chapter 1: 90 minutes Basic Concepts
  * The tester learns the basic concepts related to digital accessibility testing
  * The tester understands the different categories of disabilities
  * The tester understands the legal risks and impact of not applying accessibility to software
* Chapter 2: 75 minutes Accessibility Standards
  * The tester learns accessibility standards and manufacturer guidelines
  * The tester understands accessibility regulations
* Chapter 3: 105 minutes Accessibility testing activities
  * The tester understands the approach to accessibility testing
  * The tester learns the accessibility testing activities
  * The tester learns the most frequent and serious errors in accessibility testing
* Chapter 4: 210 minutes Accessibility testing life cycle
  * The tester learns about the accessibility testing preparation
  * The tester can identify the applicable accessibility testing tasks
  * The tester can analyze the accessibility test results
  * The tester can apply good practices to report and communicate test results
* Chapter 5: 75 minutes Assistive technology
  * The tester understands assistive technologies
  * The tester can apply assistive technologies in accessibility testing
* Chapter 6: 120 minutes Tools for accessibility testing
  * The tester understands the types to accessibility testing tools
  * The tester understands the categories of tools available
  * The tester understands how to integrate tools in software development lifecycle for accessibility testing
